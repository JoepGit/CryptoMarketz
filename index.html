LESSON 2.1
The 1–2% Rule: Never Blow Your Account
Why professional traders never risk more than 1–2% per trade. See real examples of how this rule allows you to survive 20 consecutive losses and still be in business.

Position Sizing Formula

Stop Loss Placement Strategies

R:R RatioTake Profit

LESSON 3.1
FOMO: The Silent Account Killer
Fear of Missing Out causes traders to chase pumps at tops and buy after 30%+ moves. Learn to recognise FOMO in real-time and build rules that stop you from acting on it.
BeginnerFOMODiscipline

Building a Trading Routine

Detaching from P&L

Overconfidence & Winning Streaks

AdvancedOverconfidence

The Trader's Mindset: Thinking in Probabilities
No setup wins 100% of the time. Learn to think in distributions of outcomes, accept losses as part of the system and measure success over 50–100 trade samples, not individual results.

LESSON 5.1
Higher Highs, Higher Lows & Trend Definition
A bullish trend is defined by HH and HL. A bearish trend by LH and LL. Learn to objectively identify trend structure on any timeframe and avoid counter-trend trades.
HH/HLTrendBeginner
LESSON 5.2
Break of Structure & Change of Character
BOS signals trend continuation; ChoCH signals a potential reversal. Learn to distinguish between a pullback and a genuine trend change before committing to a new direction.
BOSChoCHSMC

Order Blocks & Institutional Zones
Order blocks are the last candle before a significant move — representing institutional buy/sell orders. Learn to identify, draw and trade from these high-probability demand and supply zones.
AdvancedOrder BlocksSMC
LESSON 5.4
Liquidity Pools & Stop Hunts
Institutions hunt retail stop losses clustered below swing lows and above swing highs. Learn to identify BSL and SSL pools, anticipate liquidity sweeps and enter after the manipulation phase.
AdvancedLiquidityStop Hunt
LESSON 5.5
Fair Value Gaps & Imbalance
FVGs (three-candle imbalances) represent price moving so fast that inefficiency is left behind. Price often returns to fill these gaps. Learn to use FVGs as entry zones with precision.
AdvancedFVGImbalance

Multi-Timeframe Analysis
Use the Weekly/Daily for bias, the 4H for structure and the 1H/15m for entry triggers. Learn the top-down approach to only trade in the direction of the higher timeframe trend.

LESSON 6.1
Core vs Speculative Allocation
Split your portfolio: 60–80% in core holdings (BTC, ETH), 15–30% in mid-cap alts, and 5–10% in high-risk speculative plays. Learn why this structure protects you while capturing upside.
BeginnerAllocationBTC/ETH
LESSON 6.2
Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA)
Remove emotion by buying fixed amounts at regular intervals regardless of price. Learn the math behind DCA: it lowers your average cost in bear markets and builds conviction through volatility.
BeginnerDCAAccumulation
LESSON 6.3
Profit Taking Frameworks
Take profits systematically — not emotionally. Learn ladder selling (e.g. 25% at 2x, 25% at 3x, 25% at 5x, keep 25% long-term) and target-based exits tied to market cycle indicators.
Take ProfitLadder Selling
LESSON 6.4
Rebalancing & Portfolio Maintenance
As assets move, your allocation drifts. Learn quarterly rebalancing to maintain target weights, trim winners, add to underweight positions and reduce single-asset concentration risk.
RebalancingMaintenance

Bear Market Survival Strategy
Most investors lose 80–90% in bear markets. Learn to identify bear market conditions early, rotate to stablecoins, set buy targets for quality assets and use downtrends to accumulate at discounts.
AdvancedBear MarketStables

Compounding & Long-Term Thinking
$10,000 growing at 40% annually becomes $289,000 in 10 years. Understand the power of compounding, minimising taxes through holding periods and thinking in 4-year cycle horizons not days.

The 1–2% Rule
Never risk more than 1–2% of your total account on a single trade. $5,000 account = max $100 risk per trade.

          
Stop Losses — Always
A stop loss closes your trade automatically at a set price. Never enter a trade without one.

          
Risk/Reward Ratio
Only take trades where potential profit is at least 2–3× your potential loss. 50% win rate is profitable at 2:1.

          
Diversification
Spread your risk across positions. One bad trade should never be able to wipe your account.
